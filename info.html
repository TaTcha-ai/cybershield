Overview

This dashboard helps visualize cyber threats targeting Malaysian users between 18 to 60. It summarizes key metrics and trends in an easy-to-understand format by using year, month and date filter at the top of the dashboard.


Terms that user need to know:


-Data Compromised often referred to as a data breach, is the unauthorized access, acquisition, disclosure, or use of sensitive data. This can involve the exposure, theft, or alteration of information that should be kept private or secure. It typically occurs when someone bypasses security measures and gains unauthorized access to a system or network.


-A zero-day exploit is a type of cyberattack that takes advantage of a software vulnerability that is not known to the company that developed it yet, meaning they haven't released a patch for it. Basically, it's an attack that occurs on the very first day that the vulnerability is discovered. The term "zero-day" means that the software or hardware seller has no time to fix the problem before it starts being taken advantage of.


A mitigation method refers to actions taken to reduce the severity, impact, or likelihood of a negative event or condition.

Outcome is to determine weather the company or individual manage to prevent and stop the cyber attack or not.
